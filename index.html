Helpful Background

Let’s Catch You Up

Our story begins with the President. Of the nation. As in, Barack Obama. Specifically his 2011 State Of The Union Address in which he called for 100,000 new teachers in the fields of science, technology, engineering, and math.

That direct challenge from the leader of the free world led to the formation of 100Kin10, a non-profit with more than 150 partner organizations. In the fall of 2013 they hatched a plan to brand this entire movement in a way that spoke more directly to the young, smart STEM majors who will become many of those 100,000 new teachers. The goal was to have partners chip in to create something bigger and more amazing than any one of them could accomplish on their own.

That’s where Cultivated Wit comes in. They are a creative agency that uses comedy, technology, and design to tell stories, build community, and teach. They put in a bid to lead that branding effort, and most certainly didn’t blackmail anybody to win it.

Now everybody is married and 30+ partners have contributed money and flatware to help them begin their life together. This fall, they are expecting to birth a beautiful recruitment campaign. Okay, this metaphor is getting uncomfortable…


Campaign Overview

YOUR DAILY GRIND COULD BLOW A MIND

Now for the fun part.

The real magic of teaching is packed into the moment when a kid “gets it.” That moment of not just learning, but truly understanding. Any teacher knows the look that sweeps over a kid’s face when their mind is blown. And no subjects are more mind blowing than STEM, meaning no subject sizzles more synapses than a STEM teacher.

This campaign will create a pile of assets that drive this point home: Minds Don’t Blow Themselves. Videos, GIFs, infographics, tweets, and physical paper products that people can then photograph and post on Instagram… Distributing fun and engaging content will be the first step in uncovering the next generation of fun and engaging teachers.

Minds Don’t Blow Themselves.

That pile of assets will lead people back to a website that connects all the fun stuff back to the overall mission. It will emphasize the long term positive impact of teaching, and help young people realize that becoming a teacher is more attainable than they may realize. Part of that last step is executing an effective handoff from this campaign over to teaching programs that can actually move the needle on our goal for 100,000 excellent new teachers.

See, it’s easy. I mean, except for the hours of writing and designing and animating and programming and finding distribution partners and scheduling flights to D.C. to receive congratulations from the President, but we still have all summer for that.


Extra Goodies

WE’RE DROPPING KNOWLEDGE BOMBS

But wait! There’s more!

Beyond just the campaign, a cornerstone of this partnership is rising all boats with resources intended to make everybody better at recruiting college students and recent grads. All the participating organizations will join a workshop and receive an exhaustive guide to digital marketing among this demographic. The campaign is the fish, this is the fishing lesson. The internet is the water. Or maybe it’s the boat? You know what, we’re going to move along…

There is even more we would love to accomplish with this campaign—communities we would love to engage, content we would love to create, blimps we would love to fly over the Super Bowl. But all those things require more money. Especially the last one.
